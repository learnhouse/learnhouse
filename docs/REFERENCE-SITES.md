# Reference Sites & Moodboard — Maya Financial Coaching Storefront

> Research for Maya's Hebrew-first, editorial-leaning Webflow storefront. Audience: financially stressed women 25–50. Bar: "editorial/distinctive," not generic coach-site.
> Compiled 2026-04-19.

---

## Bucket 1 — International finance-coach / creator sites

### 1. Ramit Sethi — I Will Teach You To Be Rich
- **URL:** https://www.iwillteachyoutoberich.com
- **Category:** Personal-finance creator / course flagship
- **Patterns to steal:**
  - **"Time Tested. Proven Results"** credibility block (~20 yrs, 42,000+ students) — anchors authority without flashy claims; copy Maya can echo with her own stat.
  - **Five long-form case-study testimonials** with photo + name + concrete outcome (debt paid off, travel funded, loan retired). Each is a mini narrative, not a one-liner.
  - **Third-party validation via media** (Netflix show block) — treated as a peer section, not a logo strip.
  - Multiple CTAs that branch ("money coaching" vs "business") instead of one hard sell.
- **Anti-patterns:** copy density gets heavy below the fold; hero visual is a screenshot of the platform (weak for a personal brand — Maya should lead with a human face).

### 2. Dave Ramsey — Ramsey Solutions
- **URL:** https://www.ramseysolutions.com
- **Category:** Mass-market personal finance brand
- **Patterns to steal:**
  - **"7 Baby Steps" framework** rendered as numbered cards — turns the methodology into the hero content. Maya should name her own path (e.g., "4 שלבים לחופש כלכלי") and make it the backbone of navigation.
  - **"Which Baby Step are you on?" interactive quiz** — segments visitors and serves as a CRM-style lead-capture.
  - **Emotional testimonials** ("I cried the first time we finished a month and actually had money left over") — lead with feeling, not numbers.
  - Stats as trust signals: "30 years" / "10M+ lives changed" up high.
- **Anti-patterns:** 50+ nav items; over-commercial upsells (pros, tools, books, radio). Maya should keep nav ≤ 6 items.

### 3. The Financial Diet
- **URL:** https://thefinancialdiet.com
- **Category:** Editorial blog / media brand for women + money
- **Patterns to steal:**
  - **"The #1 destination for women to talk about money"** — single-line audience positioning in the hero. Maya needs the Hebrew equivalent.
  - **Channel cards** (Newsletter / Podcast / YouTube / Social) as the primary content module instead of a chronological blog feed — treats the brand as a media company.
  - **"Write for Us" + explicit affiliate/privacy disclosures** signal editorial integrity (subtle trust move).
  - Newsletter signup appears 2–3× with different framings down the page.
- **Anti-patterns:** hero is image-heavy without a strong headline punch; typography is safe (generic sans-serif) — Maya can outclass it with a proper serif display.

### 4. Nicole Victoria — No Budget Babe
- **URL:** https://nobudgetbabe.com
- **Category:** Debt-payoff coach / course seller (closest direct analog to Maya)
- **Patterns to steal:**
  - **Origin story as hero copy** — "$40k debt → $1M net worth by 30." Maya's own transformation/story should be the hero, not a stock shot.
  - **Two-tier sequential course architecture** ("Makin Money Moves" → "Income Multiplier") — creates a customer path, not a menu. Maya's debt course = Step 1, follow-up investing/saving course = Step 2.
  - **Press bar** ("Business Insider featured") up top.
  - Supporting micro-courses for specific pain points (negotiation, home buying).
- **Anti-patterns:** testimonials are MISSING from the homepage despite claiming 100k students — do not repeat this. Footer popup with zero context. Copy slang ("freaking," "MEGA dollars") alienates some readers; Maya's tone should be warm-editorial, not bro-ish.

### 5. Erin Lowry — Broke Millennial
- **URL:** https://brokemillennial.com
- **Category:** Author / personal-finance educator
- **Patterns to steal:**
  - **"Money on your mind?"** — conversational, anxiety-naming hero headline. Hebrew equivalent could be "יש לך כאב ראש מהחשבונות?"
  - **Lead-magnet PDF** (the #GYFLT Worksheet) capturing email above the fold.
  - **Branded hashtag / movement** (#GYFLT) turns readers into a tribe — Maya could do this in Hebrew.
  - **Press bar** (NYT, Cosmo logos) as trust proof for an author brand.
  - Book block with Amazon/B&N buttons feels editorial, not salesy.
- **Anti-patterns:** site is mostly a WordPress blog skin — typography is flat, no editorial ambition. Maya should exceed this baseline.

### 6. Vivian Tu — Your Rich BFF
- **URL:** https://yourrichbff.com
- **Category:** Gen-Z / millennial finance creator
- **Patterns to steal:**
  - **"Your Favorite Wall Street Girly"** — persona-led hero tagline that is both warm and credentialed.
  - **Credentials named inline** (U Chicago, ex-Wall Street trader) — credibility without a stuffy "As Seen In" strip.
  - **Three-pillar messaging** (Get your bag / Know your worth / Ask for more) — memorable, actionable, repeated.
  - **Green + black + yellow** palette — financial-modern but warm, not bank-blue.
- **Anti-patterns:** the three pillars repeat twice (redundant). Newsletter signup duplicated top + bottom. Social-first vibe may read too young for Maya's 25–50 target.

## Bucket 2 — Editorial / typography inspiration

### 7. Morning Brew
- **URL:** https://www.morningbrew.com
- **Category:** Newsletter media brand
- **Patterns to steal:**
  - **Plain 2-color masthead + logomark** — no decoration. Editorial confidence.
  - **Section dividers with faint borders, rounded corners, generous spacing** — each story reads as its own card.
  - **ALL-CAPS section heads in accent color**, different sizes per hierarchy level.
  - **Bold first line** of every story doubles as a headline; word count tapers to accelerate reading.
- **Apply to Maya:** each blog post should have a bold first-sentence "lede," ALL-CAPS Hebrew section labels (in a font that handles caps well — Rubik, Assistant), and faint rule-lines separating thoughts.

### 8. The Cut (New York Magazine)
- **URL:** https://www.thecut.com
- **Category:** Editorial women's culture magazine
- **Patterns to steal:**
  - **Large serif display headlines** paired with clean sans-serif body — the signature editorial contrast.
  - **Generous whitespace + asymmetric grid** — articles don't sit on a rigid grid.
  - **Pull quotes in oversized serif** break up long-form.
  - **Image + caption treatment** — captions in small italic sans, creating a "this is journalism" vibe.
- **Apply to Maya:** for blog posts, treat pull quotes as design moments. Use a serif (Frank Ruhl Libre) for display and let it breathe.

### 9. Heated — Mark Bittman / Emily Atkin (Substack)
- **URL:** https://heated.world
- **Category:** Personal newsletter with editorial posture
- **Patterns to steal:**
  - **One-line POV tagline** ("A newsletter for people who are pissed off about the climate crisis") — names the reader's emotional state.
  - **Peer testimonials from other writers** (Substack recommendations) as trust signals — Maya could do the Israeli equivalent: quotes from other Hebrew creators/journalists.
  - **Distinctive logomark** does the branding; typography stays restrained.
- **Anti-pattern:** default Substack chrome is generic — Maya's Webflow build should exceed this.

### 10. Anne of Carversville
- **URL:** https://www.anneofcarversville.com
- **Category:** Independent editorial / lifestyle publication
- **Patterns to steal:**
  - **Card-based grid with category labels** (Fashion / Jewelry / Culture / Wellness) — simple, scannable.
  - **Generous line-height and bylines** — prioritizes readability over density.
  - Clear thematic archives (not just date-based) — "Start Here" / "Archives" / topic pages.

## Bucket 3 — Israeli / Hebrew-first sites

### 11. Paamonim — פעמונים
- **URL:** https://www.paamonim.org/he/
- **Category:** Israeli non-profit for family financial guidance (closest Hebrew-native analog)
- **Patterns to steal:**
  - **Proper RTL implementation** throughout — good benchmark for Hebrew form fields, nav, and line-height.
  - **"הרשמה"-style CTAs** repeated in different contexts (workshop signup vs course signup vs newsletter).
  - **Warm-advocacy tone** ("ניהול כלכלי נכון פותח דלתות לחיים טובים ורגועים") — Maya's voice should be in this territory, not corporate bank-speak.
- **Anti-patterns:** design is utilitarian / non-profit-generic. Typography is default Hebrew system stack. Navigation is over-stuffed. Maya must go far more editorial.

### 12. Mesila — מסילה
- **URL:** https://www.mesila.org
- **Category:** Hebrew financial-coaching organization (families + businesses)
- **Patterns to steal:**
  - **Three-step methodology** (מודעות → שינוי → יישום / Awareness → Change → Implementation) — same "named path" pattern as Ramsey, in Hebrew.
  - **Tracks-not-products** framing — "family track" / "business track" reduces decision fatigue.
  - Clean RTL implementation, sticky header.
- **Anti-patterns:** generic stock photography, weak typographic hierarchy, color palette reads "NGO." Maya should out-class with editorial photography + serif display.

### 13. Rifka Lebowitz (English-for-Israel financial planner)
- **URL:** https://rifkalebowitz.com
- **Category:** Israel-based financial coach (English audience)
- **Patterns to steal:**
  - **Niche-positioning headline** — "Empowering English speakers to live a financially smarter life in Israel" — hyper-specific audience framing. Maya's could be "לנשים ישראליות שמוכנות לצאת מהחובות."
  - **Three-tier offering structure:** 1:1 consults / self-paced courses / speaking.
  - **Stats as social proof:** "25+ years, 3,200+ clients, 40,000+ group members."
  - **Pain-point copy:** "earning decent income but running on a deficit" names the exact reader.
- **Anti-patterns:** template-feeling coach scaffolding, repeated nav menus, no Hebrew integration despite Israeli market. Typography is safe/corporate.

### Hebrew typography lessons (across Bucket 3)
- Most Hebrew coach sites default to **Heebo / Assistant / Open Sans Hebrew** at a single weight — visually flat.
- Common failure: treating RTL as "flip LTR and call it done." Maya's site should design **hero, pull quotes, and cards natively in RTL** (image on the left, text on the right, eye-line starts top-right).
- Hebrew headlines look best **slightly larger and tighter-tracked** than equivalent English; generous line-height (1.5–1.7) for body.

## Hebrew-capable font pairings (editorial-warm-trustworthy)

All pairings use fonts with strong Hebrew glyph coverage. Pair one display/serif with one body/sans; keep to 2 families max.

| # | Display / Headlines | Body | Vibe | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Frank Ruhl Libre** (serif, 700/900) | **Assistant** (sans, 400/600) | Classic editorial, trustworthy | FRL is the most "magazine" Hebrew serif (based on the 1910 Frank Rühl print type). Pairs with Assistant's elegant low-contrast sans. **Recommended default.** |
| 2 | **Frank Ruhl Libre** (serif) | **Heebo** (sans) | Editorial + modern/utility | Heebo has 9 weights, strongest workhorse sans for Hebrew. Good when body text needs range (captions, labels, buttons). |
| 3 | **Rubik** (rounded sans, 700 for display) | **Heebo** (sans, 400) | Warm, approachable, not stuffy | All-sans pairing using weight + rounding for contrast. Use when serif feels too "old/institutional" for Maya's audience. |
| 4 | **David Libre** (serif) | **Rubik** (rounded sans) | Warm editorial, human | David Libre is a softer, less formal Hebrew serif than FRL — feels like a trusted friend. Rubik's rounded terminals match the warmth. |
| 5 | **Noto Serif Hebrew** (serif, display weight) | **Assistant** (sans) | Contemporary editorial | Noto Serif Hebrew is Google's modern high-contrast Hebrew serif — more "Cut / New Yorker" than FRL's "classic Israeli press." |

**Runner-ups / alternatives:**
- **Miriam Libre** — geometric, good for all-caps labels and small nav items.
- **Alef** — clean, neutral; works as an all-purpose body sans if Heebo feels too tall.
- **Suez One** — slab serif for a heavier, more modern display option (pair with Assistant).

**Avoid:** Narkisim (dated default), Arial Hebrew, and any system-stack fallbacks as primary brand fonts. Avoid italics in Hebrew (they're faux-slanted and read poorly).

**Weight / size rules for RTL:**
- Hebrew display at ~90–95% of the visual size you'd use for equivalent English headlines (Hebrew reads larger per x-height).
- Body line-height 1.55–1.7; tracking at 0 or slightly tighter on headlines.
- Never bold full paragraphs — Hebrew bold gets dense fast.

## Patterns to steal for Maya — synthesized by page

### Home
- [ ] **Hero = Maya's face + 1 sentence that names the reader's pain.** Model: Ramit's photo-led hero + Erin Lowry's "Money on your mind?" pattern. Hebrew equivalent like "החובות לא נותנים לך לישון?" followed by a warm promise.
- [ ] **Named methodology as a numbered visual block** (Ramsey Baby Steps / Mesila 3 steps). E.g., "4 שלבים לחופש כלכלי." Makes the framework ownable and gives navigation structure.
- [ ] **Stats-as-trust-signal row** (Rifka / Ramit): "X שנים, Y תלמידות, Z ש"ח חובות שנפרעו."
- [ ] **Press / media / podcast logo strip** (Broke Millennial / Rifka) — low-cost big trust.
- [ ] **Long-form testimonial block with 3–5 case studies** (Ramit pattern) — photo + name + concrete before/after number. Not a one-liner carousel.
- [ ] **Two-tier course pitch** (Nicole Victoria): debt-exit course first, investing/saving as step 2 — even if step 2 is "coming soon," show the path.
- [ ] **Email capture with a Hebrew lead magnet** (Erin Lowry #GYFLT pattern). E.g., "גיליון קסם: 30 דקות לאבחון החוב שלך."
- [ ] **Pinned CTA** on scroll ("הצטרפי לקורס") — subtle, not a huge bar.

### About
- [ ] **Origin-story-as-hero** (Nicole Victoria pattern): Maya's own debt-to-freedom narrative in first person, with specific numbers.
- [ ] **Credentials inline, not stuffed in a sidebar** (Vivian Tu pattern): certifications named in a sentence, not a logo grid.
- [ ] **Editorial portrait photography** (think The Cut, not LinkedIn). 2–3 real photos in different contexts.
- [ ] **"מה הרעיון" / philosophy block** — 2–3 short paragraphs in serif, like a magazine essay intro.
- [ ] **Second CTA to the course** at bottom (don't let About be a dead-end).

### Blog
- [ ] **Magazine grid** with category labels (Anne of Carversville / The Cut pattern). Categories like "חובות," "תכנון," "הרגלים," "סיפורים."
- [ ] **Bold first-sentence lede** in every post (Morning Brew move).
- [ ] **Oversized serif pull quotes** (The Cut move) breaking up long-form — feels editorial, not bloggy.
- [ ] **ALL-CAPS Hebrew section labels** in a sans (Rubik/Assistant) between article sections.
- [ ] **Generous line-height 1.6** + ~680px max width for readability.
- [ ] **"התחילי כאן" curated path** for new readers (TFD pattern) — pick 5 posts that onboard.
- [ ] **Newsletter signup in 3 forms**: inline mid-post, footer, and as a separate landing page.

### Course page
- [ ] **Named path as module list** with outcome per module (Ramsey-style numbered steps).
- [ ] **Case-study testimonials with before/after numbers** (Expert X / Ramit pattern) — "נכנסתי לקורס עם 70k חוב, 9 חודשים אחרי: 0."
- [ ] **FAQ stack** addressing Hebrew-speaker-specific objections (is this for ultra-Orthodox / secular / both? is there a guarantee? mobile-friendly?).
- [ ] **Scroll-triggered course-preview video** (1–2 min of Maya talking to camera) anchored near the pricing block — converts more than any bullet list.
- [ ] **Pricing transparency**: don't hide the number. One-time + payment plan.
- [ ] **Micro-commitment CTA** ("קבלי את השיעור הראשון בחינם") alongside the full-course CTA.
- [ ] **"Who this is / isn't for" block** — filters out bad fits, raises trust for good fits.

### Global / across pages
- [ ] **2-font system max** (e.g., Frank Ruhl Libre display + Assistant body).
- [ ] **Color palette**: warm neutrals + one editorial accent (deep green, terracotta, or muted navy — avoid bank-blue and teal-coach cliché).
- [ ] **Real photography** over illustration. One Maya portrait, a few environmental shots (notebook, coffee, Tel Aviv window light) — no stock.
- [ ] **RTL-first layouts** — never designed LTR then mirrored.
- [ ] **Light scroll interactions only** — fades and reveals (Good Coach pattern). No parallax zoos.
- [ ] **Nav ≤ 6 items**: קורס / אודות / בלוג / סיפורי הצלחה / חינם / אנגלית.

## Things NOT to do (collected anti-patterns)
- Generic "coach-template" SaaS feel (seen across BRIX, Wavesdesign defaults when unstyled).
- Massive nav with 30+ items (Ramsey anti-pattern).
- Testimonial-less homepage despite claiming X,000 students (No Budget Babe).
- Slang-heavy copy that alienates older reader (Your Rich BFF / No Budget Babe).
- Stock photography + default Hebrew system font (Paamonim / Mesila).
- Flipping LTR design into RTL without redesigning eye-path.
- Faux-italic Hebrew (Webflow's italic flag on Hebrew text produces bad output).
- Hiding course price behind a form.
- More than 2 font families.
- Duplicated newsletter signups without variation (Your Rich BFF, TFD to a lesser extent).
